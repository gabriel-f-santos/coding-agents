# Market Research — sizing, competitors, demand

Offer this after the founder interview has a clear problem and target. **Fan out with subagents
when available** (`Task`), one per axis, then synthesize — otherwise run inline with
`WebSearch`/`WebFetch`. Always state confidence and cite sources; flag guesses.

## Axis 1 — Market sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM)

- **TAM** (Total Addressable) — the ceiling if everyone who could, did.
- **SAM** (Serviceable Available) — the slice you can actually serve (segment, geography).
- **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable) — what you can realistically capture in 1–3 years.

Compute **both** ways and cross-check:
- **Bottom-up (preferred):** `nº de clientes potenciais × ACV (receita anual por cliente) ×
  geografia alcançável`. Defensible — you counted real customers and know pricing.
- **Top-down:** `relatório de indústria (Gartner/IDC/Statista) × % do seu nicho`. Shows the
  ceiling.
- If the two land within ~15%, assumptions are likely solid. Presenting both builds credibility
  (investors stress-test one against the other).

Rules of thumb (not universal): venture-scale wants TAM ≥ $1B; vertical SaaS can work at ~$300M
TAM if SOM capture is high (5–15%) with strong unit economics.

## Axis 2 — Competitive landscape

1. **Find competitors** from the customer's shoes: category keywords, review sites (G2,
   Capterra), forums (Reddit, communities), industry directories, Crunchbase (funding),
   SEMrush (traffic/keywords).
2. **Classify:** **direct** (same product), **indirect** (same need, different way — incl.
   spreadsheets, manual processes, "do nothing"), **future entrants**.
3. **Map** with SWOT + a feature/positioning matrix. Look for the **gap** — an underserved
   segment, an unmet job, a price/UX wedge.
4. Capture each competitor's pricing, target segment, and the wedge you'd use against them.

## Axis 3 — Demand signals (does anyone want this?)

Look for evidence people **already spend time or money** on the problem:
- Search volume / "how do I…" queries; subreddit & community threads complaining about it.
- Existing paid alternatives (even ugly ones) — paid alternatives are the strongest signal.
- Hiring/agency spend, manual workarounds, spreadsheets-as-product.

### Pre-MVP demand test (recommend it; instrument in research-instruments.md)
- **Fake door / landing page / waitlist** — advertise the not-yet-built offer, measure real
  clicks and opt-ins. **Smoke test** = whole-product landing page; **fake door** = a single
  feature inside an existing product.
- Track **CTR** (clicks on CTA ÷ unique visitors) and **opt-in rate** (% leaving email / booking
  a call). A real email or a booked call is "currency"; a thumbs-up is not.
- Note: waitlists convert far better when nurtured — founders who email every ~2 weeks convert
  ~35–42% vs ~8–12% when silent. Pre-order customers convert at ~3–4× general signups.

## Synthesis

Produce a one-page market summary: TAM/SAM/SOM (both methods + the number you'd defend), the
competitor matrix with the gap you'd attack, and the demand evidence with its strength. Feed it
back into the interview — it routinely reframes the opportunity and the riskiest assumption.
